Attack Surface Management (ASM)

Discover and reduce what attackers can see and target from the outside.

What is Attack Surface?

Imagine your company is a castle. Your "attack surface" is every place an enemy could try to get in:

üö™ Doors = Open ports and services
ü™ü Windows = Web applications
üï≥Ô∏è Secret tunnels = Shadow IT, forgotten servers
üì¨ Mailbox = Email gateways
üåê The whole perimeter = Internet-facing assets

Attack Surface Management = Finding ALL the ways in before the bad guys do!

Interview Questions

üíº Interview Q&A

Q: "What is Attack Surface Management and how does it differ from vulnerability management?"

"ASM focuses on discovering and monitoring your internet-facing assets from an external attacker's perspective, while traditional VM scans known internal assets. ASM answers 'what can attackers see?' while VM answers 'what vulnerabilities exist on our known assets?' They're complementary - ASM discovers assets, VM scans them for vulnerabilities."


Q: "How would you reduce an organization's attack surface?"

"First, discover everything using ASM tools - domains, IPs, exposed services, shadow IT. Then reduce exposure by: moving admin interfaces behind VPN, blocking unnecessary ports at the perimeter, decommissioning forgotten systems, consolidating redundant services, and implementing continuous monitoring to catch new exposures quickly. The goal is minimizing what attackers can see and target."
