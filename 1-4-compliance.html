PCI DSS v4.0

The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) applies to any organization that stores, processes, or transmits cardholder data. PCI DSS v4.0, released in March 2022, includes significant vulnerability management requirements.

HIPAA Security Rule

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) Security Rule requires covered entities and business associates to implement safeguards protecting electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI). While HIPAA doesn't prescribe specific scanning frequencies, it requires risk management processes.

Relevant HIPAA Safeguards

Safeguard Section VM Relevance
Risk Analysis Â§164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A) Identify vulnerabilities to ePHI systems
Risk Management Â§164.308(a)(1)(ii)(B) Implement measures to reduce risks to reasonable level
Protection from Malware Â§164.308(a)(5)(ii)(B) Guard against malicious software including exploits
Technical Evaluation Â§164.308(a)(8) Periodic technical/non-technical evaluation
Transmission Security Â§164.312(e)(1) Encryption vulnerabilities, SSL/TLS issues

HIPAA VM Best Practices

  • Conduct annual risk analysis including vulnerability assessment of ePHI systems
  • Implement vulnerability scanning as part of ongoing risk management
  • Document vulnerability remediation as evidence of risk reduction
  • Prioritize vulnerabilities affecting systems with ePHI
  • Include vulnerability findings in periodic technical evaluations
ğŸ’œ HIPAA Tip

HIPAA is intentionally flexible and doesn't mandate specific scanning frequencies. However, OCR (Office for Civil Rights) expects organizations to implement "reasonable and appropriate" measures. Most healthcare organizations scan monthly and remediate critical vulnerabilities within 30 days to demonstrate reasonable security.

SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley)

The Sarbanes-Oxley Act requires public companies to maintain internal controls over financial reporting. Section 404 requires management assessment and auditor attestation of these controls. IT general controls (ITGCs) including vulnerability management are often in scope.

SOX IT General Controls

Control Area VM Relevance Evidence Required
Change Management Patch deployment as system changes Change tickets, approval records, testing evidence
Computer Operations System maintenance including patching Patch schedules, scan reports, remediation logs
Access Controls Vulnerabilities exposing access controls Scan results for auth/access vulns, remediation
Program Development Application security vulnerabilities SAST/DAST scan results, secure SDLC evidence

Compliance Reporting

Auditors and compliance teams need specific evidence to validate VM controls:

Essential Compliance Documentation

  • VM Policy: Approved policy document with current effective date
  • Procedures: Documented scanning and remediation procedures
  • Scan Reports: Evidence of regular scanning per required frequency
  • Remediation Evidence: Tickets, patch records, verification scans
  • SLA Compliance: Metrics showing adherence to remediation timelines
  • Exception Records: Approved exceptions with compensating controls
  • Management Reviews: Evidence of oversight and governance

Summary

Compliance requirements vary by industry and framework but share common themes: establish a formal VM program, scan regularly with appropriate coverage, remediate within defined timelines, and maintain documentation for auditors. Building a strong VM program that exceeds minimum compliance requirements positions you for success across multiple frameworks. In the next section, we'll explore VM metrics and KPIs in detail.
