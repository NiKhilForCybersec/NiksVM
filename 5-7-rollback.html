"How do you handle a patch that causes system issues?"

Sample answer: "First, I always take a snapshot or backup before patching, so rollback is possible. If a patch causes issues, I follow a decision tree: system won't boot or critical service down means immediate rollback; performance issues mean evaluate severity first. For rollback, I use VM snapshot revert if available (fastest), or uninstall the specific update. After rollback, I document the issue, implement compensating controls if it's a critical vulnerability, and work with the vendor to understand the root cause before retrying."

Summary

Always prepare for rollback before patchingâ€”snapshots and backups are essential. Define clear rollback triggers so engineers know when to revert. After rollback, document the issue and ensure compensating controls protect against the unpatched vulnerability while you investigate.
