Complex VM concepts explained in the simplest possible terms.

What is a Vulnerability?

A vulnerability is like a broken lock on your door. It's not a burglar - it's just a way a burglar COULD get in if they find it.
What is an Exploit?

If the vulnerability is a broken lock, the exploit is the technique the burglar uses to open it. Sometimes they jiggle it a certain way, sometimes they use a special tool. The exploit is the "how to break in" instructions.
What is a Patch?

A patch is like getting a locksmith to come fix your broken lock. It's the official repair from the lock company (software vendor) that makes the lock work properly again.
What is CVSS?

CVSS is like a danger rating for vulnerabilities, from 0 to 10. Think of it like a weather warning:
üü¢ 1-3 = Nice weather, no rush
üü° 4-6 = Rain coming, bring an umbrella
üü† 7-8 = Big storm, prepare your house
üî¥ 9-10 = Hurricane! Take shelter NOW!
What is EPSS?

CVSS tells you how bad a vulnerability COULD be. EPSS tells you how likely bad guys will ACTUALLY use it soon. It's like knowing a hurricane is possible (CVSS) vs knowing one is heading your way right now (EPSS).
What is a Zero-Day?

A zero-day is like finding out burglars discovered a secret entrance to houses that nobody knew about - and they're already using it! There's no fix yet because nobody knew it existed. You have "zero days" to prepare.
What is Scanning?

Scanning is like a security guard walking through your whole building, checking every door and window to see if any locks are broken. They make a list of everything they find wrong.
Authenticated vs Unauthenticated Scan?

Unauthenticated = Looking at a house from the street. You can see the outside, but not what's inside.
Authenticated = Going inside with a key. You can open closets, check the basement, see everything!

Authenticated finds 5-10x more problems!
What is Remediation?

Remediation just means "fixing the problem." You found a broken lock? Fix it! That's remediation. You can fix it properly (patch), board up the door (workaround), or add extra security cameras (compensating control).
What is a Compensating Control?

Can't fix the broken lock right away? Add extra protection instead! Put a doorstop, add a camera, get a guard dog. These don't fix the lock, but they make it much harder for burglars. That's a compensating control.
What is Risk Acceptance?

Sometimes you know about a broken lock but decide: "This door leads to an empty closet, it's fine for now." You write it down, get your boss to agree, and accept the small risk. That's risk acceptance - knowingly living with a vulnerability.
What is an SLA?

SLA (Service Level Agreement) is like promising "we'll fix any broken locks within 24 hours." It's your promise for how fast you'll fix things. Critical locks = faster. Closet locks = can wait longer.
What is Crown Jewels?

Crown jewels are your most important things - your safe with money, your family photos, your irreplaceable stuff. In IT, it's your payment systems, customer data, domain controllers. Protect these FIRST!
What is CMDB?

CMDB (Configuration Management Database) is like a master list of everything you own - every computer, every server, who owns it, where it is, what it does. You need this list to know what to protect!
What is Shadow IT?

Shadow IT is like finding out someone in your family installed a doggy door you didn't know about. It's technology people set up without telling IT. Surprise! Now there's another way in that nobody was protecting.
What is Attack Surface?

Your attack surface is every possible way someone could try to break in - every door, window, doggy door, chimney, air vent. The bigger your house (network), the more entry points (attack surface) you have to watch.
What is MTTR?

MTTR (Mean Time To Remediate) = "On average, how long does it take us to fix a broken lock after we find it?" Lower is better! If your MTTR is 30 days, you're leaving locks broken for a month on average.
What is False Positive?

A false positive is when the security guard says "this lock is broken!" but actually it's fine - they made a mistake. You check it, it works perfectly. False alarm!
What is CVE?

CVE is like a unique ID for every discovered vulnerability. "CVE-2021-44228" is the official name for Log4Shell, like how every person has a Social Security Number. When someone says a CVE number, everyone knows exactly which vulnerability they mean.
What is CISA KEV?

CISA KEV is the government's "Most Wanted" list for vulnerabilities. If a vulnerability is on this list, it means bad guys are DEFINITELY using it right now. Fix these first - no excuses!
